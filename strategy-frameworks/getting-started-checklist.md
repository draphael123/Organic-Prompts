# Getting Started: Add-On Promotion Initiative

A practical checklist to help you get moving on your add-on promotion plan.

## Immediate Actions (This Week)

### 1. Information Gathering
- [ ] **Current Performance**: Pull data on current add-on promotion (if any exists)
  - What add-ons are available?
  - What's the current conversion rate?
  - How are they currently mentioned/promoted?
  
- [ ] **Compliance Review**: Understand the rules
  - Review FDA guidelines for prescription medication promotion
  - Check platform policies (TikTok, Instagram, etc.) for medical content
  - Identify required disclaimers/disclosures
  - Document approval process needed

- [ ] **Team Assessment**: Gauge readiness
  - Talk to 2-3 team members about their comfort level
  - Identify concerns or questions
  - Understand what they'd need to feel confident

- [ ] **Business Context**: Understand the "why"
  - What's the business goal? (revenue, customer value, etc.)
  - What's the priority level?
  - What resources are available?
  - Who are the key stakeholders?

### 2. Stakeholder Alignment
- [ ] Identify who needs to be involved:
  - [ ] Compliance/legal team
  - [ ] Content team members
  - [ ] Leadership/sponsor
  - [ ] Analytics/tracking team
  - [ ] Product team (to understand add-ons)

- [ ] Schedule initial conversations
- [ ] Document concerns, requirements, and expectations

### 3. Quick Wins Planning
- [ ] Identify the easiest, lowest-risk approach to start:
  - Which content creator is most comfortable/experienced?
  - What content format is simplest to test?
  - What's the smallest meaningful test?

## Planning Questions to Answer

### Business Questions
- What's the primary goal? (Revenue, awareness, customer value?)
- What's a realistic target for the first 30/60/90 days?
- How will success be measured?
- What's the budget/resources allocated?

### Content Questions  
- How does this fit into current content strategy?
- What's the right frequency? (Every post? Once a week? Situational?)
- What tone/approach resonates with your audience?
- How do we balance promotion with value/education?

### Team Questions
- What training is needed?
- What templates or resources would help?
- How should performance be evaluated?
- What support is needed from leadership?

### Compliance Questions
- What disclaimers are required?
- What claims can/can't be made?
- What approval process exists?
- What happens if something goes wrong?

## Week 1 Deliverable

Create a simple document answering:

1. **What are we doing?** (1-2 sentences)
2. **Why are we doing it?** (Business case)
3. **How will we start?** (Pilot plan)
4. **What do we need?** (Resources, approvals, etc.)
5. **How will we measure success?** (Key metrics)

## Week 2-3: Pilot Design

Once you have the foundation:

- [ ] Design a small pilot (1-2 creators, 1-2 weeks)
- [ ] Create content guidelines/templates
- [ ] Set up tracking/measurement
- [ ] Run the pilot
- [ ] Gather feedback

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- ❌ Skipping compliance review
- ❌ Not getting team buy-in
- ❌ Starting too big/ambitious
- ❌ Not defining success metrics
- ❌ Not planning for pushback/concerns

## Questions to Help You Think Deeper

1. **If this goes perfectly, what does it look like in 3 months?**
   - Helps you visualize success

2. **What's the worst-case scenario and how do we prevent it?**
   - Risk mitigation thinking

3. **What would make this easy for the team?**
   - Removes friction

4. **What would make you uncomfortable about this initiative?**
   - Surface concerns early

5. **What's the smallest test that would tell us something useful?**
   - Start small, learn fast

---

**Your First Step**: Pick 2-3 items from "Immediate Actions" and start there. Don't try to answer everything at once - gather information, then plan.
