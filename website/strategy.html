ðŸš€ Add-On Promotion Strategy Framework

Use this framework to structure your thinking about promoting add-ons through organic content.

Key Questions to Answer First

1. Current State Assessment

  • What add-ons are currently being promoted (if any)?
  • How are they currently being promoted?
  • What's the current conversion rate for add-ons vs. main products?
  • What content formats are you using (posts, videos, stories, etc.)?
  • What's your team's comfort level with promoting prescription medications?

2. Compliance & Regulations

  • What are the legal/regulatory requirements for promoting prescription medications?
  • What disclaimers or disclosures are required?
  • Are there platform-specific rules (TikTok, Instagram, etc.) for medical content?
  • Do you have a compliance review process?
  • What happens if content violates regulations?

3. Audience & Messaging

  • Who is your target audience for add-ons?
  • What language/messaging resonates with them?
  • What concerns or objections might they have?
  • How do you balance education vs. promotion?
  • What's the appropriate tone for sensitive medical topics?

4. Content Strategy

  • How do add-ons naturally fit into your content?
  • What content formats work best for your audience?
  • How often should add-ons be mentioned?
  • Should it be subtle integration or explicit promotion?
  • What's your content approval process?

5. Goals & Success Metrics

  • What are you trying to achieve? (awareness, conversions, revenue)
  • How will you measure success?
  • What's a realistic target?
  • What's your baseline to compare against?
  • How will you track performance?

ðŸ“‹ Planning Framework

Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2)

Questions to answer:

  • What do we need to research or clarify before we start?
  • Who needs to be involved in this initiative?
  • What resources or training does the team need?
  • What are the guardrails/boundaries we must work within?

Actions:

  • Review compliance requirements
  • Assess current content performance
  • Identify team concerns/questions
  • Define success metrics
  • Create content guidelines

Phase 2: Pilot/Testing (Week 3-4)

Questions to answer:

  • What's the smallest test we can run to learn quickly?
  • Which content creator(s) should test this first?
  • What's a safe, low-risk way to start?
  • How will we gather feedback?

Actions:

  • Select pilot content creators
  • Create test content pieces
  • Set up tracking/measurement
  • Run initial tests
  • Gather data and feedback

Phase 3: Refinement (Week 5-6)

Questions to answer:

  • What did we learn from the pilot?
  • What worked? What didn't?
  • What adjustments need to be made?
  • How do we scale what works?

Actions:

  • Analyze pilot results
  • Refine approach based on learnings
  • Update guidelines/templates
  • Plan broader rollout

Phase 4: Scale (Week 7+)

Questions to answer:

  • How do we roll this out to the full team?
  • What training or resources are needed?
  • How do we maintain quality and compliance?
  • How do we continue to optimize?

Actions:

  • Team training/kickoff
  • Provide templates/resources
  • Ongoing support and feedback
  • Regular performance reviews

Immediate Actions (This Week)

1. Information Gathering

  • Current Performance: Pull data on current add-on promotion (if any exists)
    • What add-ons are available?
    • What's the current conversion rate?
    • How are they currently mentioned/promoted?
  • Compliance Review: Understand the rules
    • Review FDA guidelines for prescription medication promotion
    • Check platform policies (TikTok, Instagram, etc.) for medical content
    • Identify required disclaimers/disclosures
    • Document approval process needed
  • Team Assessment: Gauge readiness
    • Talk to 2-3 team members about their comfort level
    • Identify concerns or questions
    • Understand what they'd need to feel confident

Week 1 Deliverable

Create a simple document answering:

  1. What are we doing? (1-2 sentences)
  2. Why are we doing it? (Business case)
  3. How will we start? (Pilot plan)
  4. What do we need? (Resources, approvals, etc.)
  5. How will we measure success? (Key metrics)

ðŸ’¡ Questions to Help You Think Deeper

  1. If this goes perfectly, what does it look like in 3 months?
    Helps you visualize success
  2. What's the worst-case scenario and how do we prevent it?

  3. What would make this easy for the team?

  4. What would make you uncomfortable about this initiative?
